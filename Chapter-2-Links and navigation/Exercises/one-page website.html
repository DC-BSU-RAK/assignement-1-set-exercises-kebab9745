The Queen's Gambit

The Queen's Gambit tells the story of Beth Harmon, a young orphan in the 1950s who discovers an extraordinary talent for chess. As she navigates the competitive world of professional chess, she battles personal demons and societal expectations in her pursuit of becoming the world's greatest player.

Key Info

  • Genre: Period Drama, Coming-of-age
  • Created by: Scott Frank and Allan Scott
  • Starring: Anya Taylor-Joy
  • Released in: 2020
  • Number of Episodes: 7

Cast

  • Anya Taylor-Joy as Beth Harmon
  • Bill Camp as Mr. Shaibel, the janitor who teaches Beth chess.
  • Marielle Heller as Alma Wheatley, Beth's adoptive mother.
  • Thomas Brodie-Sangster as Benny Watts, a cocky but brilliant chess grandmaster who becomes an ally.
  • Harry Melling as Harry Beltik, one of Beth's early chess opponents and later a friend.
  • Moses Ingram as Jolene, Beth's steadfast and supportive friend from the orphanage.

Key Characters

  • Beth Harmon: A fiercely intelligent and independent young woman with a prodigious talent for chess. Orphaned at a young age, she finds solace and purpose in the strategic complexities of the game, but struggles with addiction and the emotional challenges of her past. Her journey is marked by her relentless drive to succeed in a male-dominated field.
  • Mr. Shaibel: The quiet and observant janitor at the Methuen Home orphanage. He is the first to recognize Beth's unique aptitude for chess and becomes her initial mentor, teaching her the fundamentals of the game in the basement. His gruff exterior hides a caring nature and a pivotal role in shaping Beth's early life.
  • Alma Wheatley: Beth's adoptive mother, a fragile and artistic woman seeking companionship and a sense of purpose. Initially overwhelmed by Beth's intensity, she eventually forms a complex and sometimes co-dependent relationship with her, supporting Beth's chess career while navigating her own personal struggles.
  • Benny Watts: A charismatic and self-assured American chess prodigy. He initially appears as a rival to Beth but later becomes a crucial mentor and friend, helping her to refine her skills and understand the higher levels of competitive chess. Their dynamic is marked by both intellectual sparring and a growing mutual respect.
  • Harry Beltik: One of Beth's early serious chess opponents. Their initial rivalry evolves into a friendship as Harry recognizes Beth's exceptional talent. He provides her with support and analysis as she progresses in her career, demonstrating a more grounded and analytical approach to the game.
  • Jolene: Beth's loyal and pragmatic friend from the Methuen Home. Jolene provides Beth with emotional stability and a connection to her past. She reappears later in Beth's life, offering crucial support and a reminder of the importance of human connection beyond the chessboard.

Reviews

The Queen's Gambit was widely praised by critics and audiences alike for its engaging narrative, the compelling portrayal of its protagonist, and its stylish production design. Many lauded Anya Taylor-Joy's performance as captivating and the series for making the world of competitive chess both accessible and thrilling.

  • Metacritic: Received a generally high Metascore, signifying universal acclaim from critics.
